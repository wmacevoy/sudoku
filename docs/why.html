Why Do Sudoku “By Hand”?

Work through each section to understand.

Format: unfold each block in order Print

Block 1 — Easy (web, with hints)

locked

Open the Sudoku assistant with Help on. Play one easy puzzle. Don’t overthink it—just click around.

What to notice (optional)
  • The tool’s pencil marks and “only” positions
  • How often you rely on highlights vs. your own working memory

After you finish

  • Did that feel like solving a puzzle, or like pushing buttons?
  • What was your mind up to? Where was your attention?
This unlocks Block 2. Goal: feel the “mindless mode.”

Block 2 — Easy (paper, no hints)

locked

Generate an easy puzzle, disable Help, print it, and solve it with pencil.

  • Cross out, annotate, backtrack. Own the state.

Discussion prompts

  • What do you remember about solving the puzzle from playig the web version?
  • Now what is your mind up to?
This unlocks Block 3. Goal: feel the “workout mode.”

Block 3 — Easy (web again, with hints)

locked

Play another easy puzzle online with Help on.

What changed?

  • What do you notice now that you didn’t notice the first easy web try in Block 1?
  • Do you still click mindlessly, or do you think about why one option works and others do not?
This unlocks Block 4. Goal: experience the contrast.

Block 4 — Medium (web, with hints)

locked

Play one medium puzzle online.

Discussion prompts

  • What were you noticing now?
  • Which strategies did you consciously choose, not just follow?
This unlocks Block 5. Goal: see the ceiling of assistance.

Block 5 — Medium (paper, no hints)

locked

Generate and print a medium puzzle. Solve it by hand. No tool assistance.

Debrief

  • How did the mental load compare to the web version?
  • How did you manage unwinding from trying something out that failed?
Unlocks the wrap-up blocks below. Goal: feel the full “manual” workout.

The Lesson

locked

To stay physically fit, you must at least sometimes use the stairs even when there is an elevator. Or you will eventually be unable to use the stairs with many other consequences.

Computers are elevators for your brain. But if that's all you do, your brain will be unfit. LLM's make this especially true. They are fantastic for efficiency, but dependence leaves your mind unfit. You need to sometimes choose to use the mental stairs.

Sudoku is a push-up for your attention and logic brain. For it to work, you have to do the push up. Watching a hockey game is not exercise.

Here are other parts of your brain you should think about exercising:

  • Creativity
  • Social intelligence
  • Attention

These are all parts of a healthy life, but the last one is crucial. The modern world is a fine-tuned assult on your attention, which it actively works to erode. If you don't exercise your attention, nothing else really matters.

Sudoku on paper exercises attention and logic. Work one out with a friend to build social intelligence. Thinking of and doing other exercises for you and your companion and now you are working on creativity.

These habits add up—over time they shape how you think.

This unlocks your healthy mind.
